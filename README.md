# ComplianceNav-AI
Compliance teams at Canadian financial institutions are navigating an expanding, overlapping set of AI governance frameworks — PIPEDA/Bill C-27, AIRAP/AIRA, OSFI model risk guidance, and internal AI ethics policies. 
Compliance teams at Canadian financial institutions are navigating an expanding, overlapping set of AI governance frameworks — PIPEDA/Bill C-27, AIRAP/AIRA, OSFI model risk guidance, and internal AI ethics policies. Today, interpreting these frameworks requires specialist knowledge that is not democratised across business units, legal teams, or product managers. The result is slow, inconsistent, and risk-prone compliance navigation.
GenAI tools that ignore this regulatory context are actively dangerous in a banking environment. What is needed is a purpose-built, RAG-powered compliance agent that retrieves authoritative answers from the actual regulatory documents — with explicit transparency about its confidence, its sources, and when a human expert must be consulted.
